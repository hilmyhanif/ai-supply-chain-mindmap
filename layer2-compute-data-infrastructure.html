01 — Definition

What Is Compute & Data Infrastructure?

Layer 2 encompasses the operational backbone that enables AI systems to train on vast datasets and serve users worldwide. Four interconnected subsystems form its core:

⚡
Compute Infrastructure

GPU / TPU Clusters & HPC

Powerful servers and accelerator arrays that perform AI computation. Training a large neural model may involve thousands of GPU/TPU chips across many servers, all interconnected by ultra-fast 400 Gbps or higher links to act as a single supercomputer.

🗄️
Data Infrastructure

Storage, Pipelines & Governance

Storage systems and pipelines that handle data at scale — tiered systems of fast SSDs, large-capacity HDD arrays, and object storage, often spread across multiple sites. Advanced distributed file systems and streaming platforms continually feed training data while enforcing governance and security.

🌐
Connectivity

Fiber Backbone & Subsea Cables

High-bandwidth networks linking systems and regions. About 99% of international data traffic flows through submarine fiber cables — the Internet's circulatory system. Data centers are also woven together via terrestrial fiber and Internet Exchange Points worldwide.

🔋
Energy & Cooling

Power Supply & Thermal Management

AI data centers can draw 10–50 MW each — requiring stable electric grids and advanced cooling from chilled air containment to direct liquid cooling for high-density AI racks. Without sufficient power and cooling, even the most advanced chips would overheat or throttle.

03 — Indonesia's Position

Indonesia: Current Standing & Strategic Potential

Indonesia is at an inflection point for AI compute and data infrastructure. The country's 270+ million population — most coming online via mobile — generates enormous data and creates a large market for AI-driven services. Its geographic position astride key maritime routes makes it ideal for hosting data hubs that provide redundancy to the Singapore route.

Key recent milestones signal serious intent: In 2025, the national research agency BRIN, in partnership with South Korea, launched a new HPC center in Jakarta as part of a $30 million, five-year ASEAN-Korea cooperation project. Meanwhile, Google Cloud (2020), AWS (2021), and Microsoft Azure (2022–25) all opened Jakarta regions — bringing world-class GPU infrastructure onto Indonesian soil.

Most significantly, Indonesia is partnering with Oracle to establish Southeast Asia's largest GPU cloud cluster, expected to house tens of thousands of GPUs — aligned with Indonesia's Digital Vision 2045 roadmap and targeting education, healthcare, and smart city applications.

On the data governance front, Indonesia enacted its first comprehensive Personal Data Protection Law in 2022, analogous to Europe's GDPR — a critical foundation for building AI training datasets ethically and securely.

04 — Strategic Roadmap

Six Strategies for Indonesia's Layer 2 Advancement

A multi-pronged strategy spanning investment, policy reform, talent development, and international collaboration is required to elevate Indonesia's position in global AI compute infrastructure.

01
Planning

National AI Infrastructure Masterplan

Formulate a dedicated masterplan with targets for compute capacity, connectivity, and data capabilities over the next decade. Prioritize establishing a National AI Supercomputing Center (expanding on BRIN–Korea HPC) with petaflop-scale access for universities, startups, and government. Offer tax holidays and accelerated depreciation for hyperscale data center campuses. Designate industrial-grade power zones near substations or power plants as data center parks with pre-laid fiber and streamlined permits.

02
Connectivity

Enhance Connectivity & Network Resilience

Fully implement recent reforms on submarine cable landing and repair. Establish an Indonesia Cable Authority as a single-window regulator. Encourage additional cable routes (Java–Australia, links to Japan and India). Extend the Palapa Ring with spur networks to smaller islands. Strengthen Internet Exchange Points in Jakarta, Surabaya, and Medan to keep local traffic domestic, reducing latency and cost. Foster competition in broadband markets to reduce Indonesia's ranking of 131st in affordability.

03
Energy

Power AI Infrastructure Sustainably

Facilitate renewable Power Purchase Agreements (PPAs) for data center operators or allow direct investment in captive solar/wind farms. Work with PLN to build dedicated high-voltage feeders to major data center clusters with battery storage for peak-shaving. Establish "Green Data Center Parks" near geothermal or hydro resources in North Sumatra or Sulawesi with pre-laid fiber and ready permits. Provide grants for direct liquid cooling adoption. Data centers may account for 20–30% of Indonesia's power demand — grid planning must begin now.

04
Talent

Talent Development & Retention

Introduce specialized "Cloud and Big Data Engineering" university tracks combining distributed systems, database management, and data center HVAC/power systems. Fund 6-month internship programs at Google, Microsoft, or AWS data centers abroad — conditional on return commitment. Establish a National AI Research Institute employing top AI PhDs with access to the national supercomputer, creating world-class research opportunities domestically. Structure the Oracle AI Center partnership to include local training programs so Indonesians operate and innovate on those facilities.

05
Data

Data Ecosystem & Governance

Create a centralized open data platform hosting non-sensitive anonymized datasets (mobility, satellite imagery, public health) to spur local AI model development. Enforce the 2022 Personal Data Protection Law with clear implementation guidelines to build enterprise trust. Consider an Indonesian Cloud and AI Alliance — a forum for government, local IT firms, and foreign cloud providers to agree on data handling standards, cybersecurity protocols, and interoperability requirements. Use data residency rules strategically to ensure digital sovereignty without stifling global services.

06
Collaboration

International & Regional Partnerships

Pursue an NVIDIA or AMD AI hardware incubator for hands-on training with the latest accelerators. Coordinate with ASEAN neighbors on an "ASEAN Digital Backbone" — resilient regional internet with shared cable protection agreements and a potential joint supercomputing grid. Engage Japan (JICA), the US International Development Finance Corp, and EU bodies for co-financing of cable routes and disaster-resilient data hubs. Position Indonesia as a "reliable partner" in diversifying AI infrastructure away from overly concentrated hubs — emphasizing political stability, large consumer base, and improving regulation.

05 — Ecosystem Support

Building the Supporting Ecosystem

Achieving Layer 2 goals is a team effort — requiring synchronized action across government, industry, academia, and international partners. If any single piece lags, the whole effort slows.

🏛️
Government Leadership

Enabling Policy & Public Investment

Invest in public goods — backbone networks and research centers. Establish a National Digital Transformation Office to coordinate across ministries. Government agencies should be early adopters of AI infrastructure, using local cloud and HPC for e-governance and smart city projects as "anchor clients" that set security and energy efficiency standards for procurement.

🤝
Public–Private Partnerships

Shared Investment in Major Projects

Government provides land and power tariff discounts for new data center parks; private consortia build and operate. Tap Indonesia's INA sovereign wealth fund or infrastructure bonds for data center development. Tech companies should adopt university ICT programs. Long-term stable returns from cloud lease agreements make digital infrastructure suitable for pension fund co-investment.

📋
Industry Associations

Standards & Knowledge Sharing

IDPRO (Indonesia Data Center Association) and ASA (AI Society of Indonesia) should develop local standards aligned with global ISO frameworks but tailored to tropical conditions. Interface with Uptime Institute, Open Compute Project, and similar bodies to keep Indonesian facilities world-class. Host annual AI Infrastructure conferences in Jakarta to bring global expertise to Indonesian audiences.

🎓
Education & Academia

Research-to-Industry Pipeline

Update curricula and provide grants for applied research in Indonesian NLP, network optimization for the Palapa Ring, and tropical cooling solutions. Establish university-industry incubators where startups doing AI-driven agriculture or health analytics can access the national supercomputer via subsidized compute credits. Embed students in real-world projects with telcos and cloud providers.

🌏
Regional Partners

ASEAN & Global Collaboration

Engage Japan (JICA), US (DFC), and EU for joint investments in cable routes and data hubs. Propose an ASEAN AI Infrastructure Working Group for green data center knowledge in tropical environments and cross-border data flow standards. Participate in OECD AI policy discussions and the Global Partnership on AI (GPAI) to remain current on trends informing domestic build-out.

🔒
Security & Trust

Cybersecurity & Governance Frameworks

BSSN and Kominfo must collaborate on a National Cybersecurity Operations Center monitoring cloud and data center threats. Require critical data centers to adhere to regular security audits and certifications. Align with international data governance norms at G20 digital ministers forums to assure global partners that Indonesia is a stable, trusted link in the AI infrastructure chain.

06 — Conclusion

The Path Forward: From Consumer to Hub

Indonesia is at a decisive inflection point. The ingredients for success are converging — improving networks, early HPC projects, a surging data center sector, and strong policy focus on digital growth. The country's potential to become a major regional hub for AI computation is real.

In concrete terms, success would mean that in 5–10 years, Indonesia could host multiple world-class data centers totaling 1+ GW of capacity, one or two supercomputing centers ranking in the regional top tier, a vibrant domestic cloud service industry, and high-speed connectivity linking all major economic centers of the archipelago.

"Countries that recognize AI infrastructure as fundamental national infrastructure — like roads or electricity — will allocate resources accordingly. Indonesia is moving in this direction; the key is to maintain momentum."
Synthesis — Layer 2 Analysis Report · 2025

Fortifying Layer 2 is not just about technology — it is about positioning Indonesia in the future global economy. As AI becomes integral to finance, farming, healthcare, and defense, countries with strong compute and data foundations will have an edge in innovation and productivity. With strategic focus and collaborative effort, Indonesia can evolve from primarily a consumer of imported cloud services into an indispensable node of the global AI network — hosting data, crunching numbers, and connecting markets — thereby securing its ambition to be a leading digital economy by its centennial in 2045.
